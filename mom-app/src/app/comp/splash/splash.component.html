About Me

Hello. I would like to introduce myself to you. I was born and raised in Ohio. My mother and grandmother were always making things for gifts or their own use. When I went to the grocery store with my mother, she would buy a coloring book for me when I was young. I loved to color and would complete book after book. Grandma K. had a blackboard and when we would visit her, I would take the chalk and make a picture. One picture that I really remember was that of a gray goose. She kept that picture on the front porch and that is where it remained while she lived in the house. Years later, it was gone but was in our memories. In Home Economics, I was introduced to the sewing machine and created an apron which looked like Wonder Bread wrapping with blue, red and yellow dots. I also moved on to knitting in 7th grade but always would crochet things more. Scarves were the most popular and at Christmas everyone got a new scarf for the Ohio winters which were pretty harse. When I was attending school, I had very few store bought clothes and my mother made most of my wardrobe, including my prom dress. She was quite a seamstress and I learned much from her.

Decades later I began to cross stitch. Started with small squares of fabric and letters or whatever pattern I could find in my mother's collection of craft books. Now I have several cross stitch Christmas stockings which are a combination of following very detailed illustrations and instructions. I also made the fabric shape of the stocking and used the sewing machine to complete. I have made presents for teachers and friends over the years.

School gifts were also completed and for Christmas, I made a reindeer which had candy canes inserted into the body as their antlers. A picture of the finished product is included in my website.

I have numerous afghans for decoration and warmth in the winter. Baby cocoons are crocheted to keep small people warm. The cocoon pattern spoke to me as a way to keep an infant snuggled and help them feel more secure. These are included in my body of works.

When I was dating my husband-to-be, his father used to say crocheting and making things were my mental therapy. He was right! I was in the nursing field when I was young and crafts were a way that I could relax. Nursing is a position I enjoyed for 42 years working at major hospitals in all areas. I started in ICU's, (Surgical and Neurologic) and moved to the Emergency Room, Diagnostic Imaging, Cardiac Catherization Lab, every surgical and medical floor including Pediatrics in my career. When I would come home from a 8 or 12 hour shift, I would pull out my most recent project and work for a few hours to decompress.

I am a mother of three, 2 sons and 1 daughter. I have been blessed with 3 beautiful grandchildren. Life has been good and I currently enjoy retirement with my husband of 45 years.

Thank you for looking at my website! I hope you see something that interests you.
